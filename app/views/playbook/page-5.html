OnBoarding is the process whereby we receive Catalogue Solutions into compliance for the first time (thereafter further assurance is either as needed or as regularly scheduled within the Standard).

Onboarding

The process for OnBoarding is as set out in an Ancillary document to the Catalogue Agreement; it consists of 4 Steps;

  1. Catalogue Agreement execution and Catalogue Solution registration, signing the agreement and registering your product (Capabilities and User Stories proposed)
  2. Capability Assessment, this is the process of establishing which Capabilities and User Stories the Solution meets.
  3. Standards Assurance, this is the process of establishing compliance with each of the Standards linked to the User Stories mapped. Each Standard has its own compliance regime with the whole flow controlled by process and tooling within the Catalogue OnBoarding team.
  4. Catalogue Listing Creation, this is the process of reviewing the information created through the OnBoarding process and creating the master data sheet for that Catalogue Solution

OnBoarding activity maps to the nature of Standards, therefore there is a block of overarching product Standards which typically require supplier submission of information and supporting documentation which is then reviewed and interrogated by an subject matter expert (SME), there are national services for which evidence of connection suffices, there are technical testers to test interoperability services and there are data and information specialists who check the implementation of Data Standards (such as the Core Information Standard or SNOMED).

We have internal teams who are able to conduct assurance on behalf of the Catalogue, and we also engage external parties to conduct assurance on our behalf via a delegation right under the Agreement.

Where external parties are engaged we audit their assurance processes and can contract with them to provide assurance services under specified conditions.

Delegated parties

Delegated parties may be;

  • Interop Standards owners outside NHS Digital (see SCI Gateway Standard)
  • they may be recognised Standards assessor bodies such as ISO accreditors (where these are incorporated into Standards)
  • they may be parties who we have contracted with in order to provide us with scaled resource.

Ensuring adequate support and access to assuring parties during the OnBoarding process is key to supporting a good supplier experience and maximising adherence to Catalogue Agreement requirements.
