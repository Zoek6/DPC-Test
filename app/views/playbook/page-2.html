Procurement contact

The DCS Catalogue or device is a Condition Precedent, placed in a procurement contract (either FW or Call Off / base contract) requiring the Supplier to be a signatory to the Catalogue Agreement and Compliant under its terms, and the Catalogue Agreement itself.

The Catalogue Agreement is an evergreen relational contract, there are no paid transactions occurring within it. The terms are therefore applied to all signatories, continuously, without any requirement for payment from any party; which includes the placement, application and assurance of Standards development under the Roadmap. Framework Authorities consider the nature of their markets, the level of future Standards development and the priority of that work and have discretion regarding any supplementary payments for Standards work.

Capabilities and Standards

The DCS sets out a Capabilities and User Stories library, which is linked to a set of Catalogue Solution Standards. Suppliers submit their Catalogue Solutions against these Capabilities and User Stories, submitting video evidence, and are then assured against the Standards that are required by the Capabilities and User Stories accepted. Once fully assured, the Supplier creates a Catalogue Solution Listing and is considered Catalogue Compliant.

NHS Digital facilitates the ongoing publication of a Roadmap which describes changes to the Capabilities and User Stories library, specifically the addition of Standards or new versions of Standards. Typically a Roadmap is managed at domain level (for instance Primary Care is a domain, Adult Social Care is a domain) because domains tend to have different commissioning structures, different digital maturity, different funding and policy focus; however this is not necessarily fixed and reflects internal customer governance.

Frameworks

Frameworks and other procurement products (e.g. DPS) can apply for access to the Catalogue (this essentially means installation of the Condition Precedent by agreement with NHS Digital). There are currently at least 5 Frameworks and DPS attached to the Catalogue and there is no real upper limit to the numbers that can be supported.

Some Frameworks are also placed on the Buying Catalogue which has a set of Marketing Pages, a comparison tool and an Order and Contract builder which is integrated with the NHS Digital Finance department and supports central invoicing.
